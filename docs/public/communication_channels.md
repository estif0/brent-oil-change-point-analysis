- **Time Horizon**: Quarterly to annual planning cycles
- **Key Questions**:

3. **Event Analysis** (3-4 pages): Major events and their quantified impacts
4. **Risk Assessment** (2-3 pages): Volatility patterns, forward-looking considerations

**Visualizations**: Clean charts with clear labels, conservative color schemes

**Format**: Virtual presentation (45-60 minutes)  
**Frequency**: Quarterly or upon major market events  
**Content**:

**Recording**: Available for on-demand viewing

**Topics Covered**:

**Format**: Brief email notifications  
**Trigger**: Detection of new significant change points  
**Content**:

## Communication Best Practices

### Universal Guidelines

1. **Tailor Language to Audience**
   - Investors: Financial terminology
   - Policymakers: Policy implications
   - Technical: Statistical rigor
   - General public: Simple analogies

2. **Emphasize Uncertainty**
   - Always present credible intervals, not just point estimates
   - Use phrases like "approximately," "likely," "suggests"
   - Avoid absolute claims

3. **Distinguish Correlation from Causation**
   - Use "associated with" rather than "caused by"
   - Acknowledge alternative explanations
   - Present evidence strength appropriately

4. **Visualizations**
   - Clean, uncluttered charts
   - Clear axis labels and legends
   - Consistent color schemes
   - Appropriate chart types for data

5. **Transparency**
   - Disclose methodology limitations
   - Provide access to technical details
   - Acknowledge data sources
   - State conflicts of interest if any

### Key Messages Across All Channels

**Core Message**: "We use advanced statistical methods to identify when and how major world events have historically affected oil prices, helping stakeholders make informed decisions."

**Supporting Messages**:
1. Bayesian approach provides uncertainty quantification
2. Analysis covers 35+ years of oil price history
3. 17 major events analyzed with quantified impacts
4. Findings inform but don't predict future prices
5. Methodology is rigorous, peer-reviewable, and transparent

## Crisis Communication Protocol

### Responding to Major Market Events

When a significant change point is detected in real-time or a major event occurs:

1. **Internal Alert** (within 2 hours): Notify internal team
2. **Client Alert** (within 6 hours): Email to premium clients with preliminary analysis
3. **Dashboard Update** (within 12 hours): Update with latest data and initial findings
4. **Detailed Analysis** (within 48 hours): Comprehensive report for clients
5. **Public Communication** (within 1 week): Press release, blog post, or media interviews
6. **Follow-up** (1 month): Updated analysis with refined estimates
